Who is Ramtin?

A musician who is mostly passionate about film & video game music, currently studying Music Technology Master's at HKU university in the Netherlands. After graduation in Physics BSc. and being a software developer in a few startups, started composing Cinematic style Hybrid-Orchestral musics. In recent years he moved to live in a very special place, the Hormuz island. It was there that all his previous, completely material views changed, and he became more involved in Art, Philosophy and Spirituality.

MEDIA SCORINGS

WESTWORLD SCORING COMPETITION 2020

Hosted by Spitfireaudio and HBO, a 5 minutes clip of the television show from episode 3 season 5 where provided to be scored, with its original soundtrack removed.

In this scene, Caleb (Aaron Paul) is on a psychedelic drug unintentionally, and as his trip is being started, he is also falling in love with Dolores (Evan Rachel Wood). The scene is full of chasing and gun fighting actions, and because of the trip, it has lots of mood changes.

What was the criteria?

The criteria was stated to be:

“enhancement of the viewing experience, creative inventiveness, adding emotion, and the ability to add to the storytelling of the clip.”

What did I do?

Although the result speaks for itself, there are a few technical features worth to mention:

  • Creating Musical Theme: Although the whole scene was only 5 minutes long, I introduced and incorporated a theme throughout the scene up to the ending.
  • Dynamic mood & Instrumentation: Caleb was experiencing mood changes, and in some scenes the picture appears to be focusing more on his emotions for Dolores, and then back to the gun fight and chasing.
  • Dynamic Rhythm & Tempo: Since the scene contained lots of fast actions, making the music to be more synced to the shots boosted the excitement of the viewer, and it required many changes of rhythm and tempo throughout the scene.

The Competition’s Results

I did not win a place,
and there were lots of controversy around the results and the judgment procedure.

Initially, it was mentioned in the Spitfire Audio’s website that the judges were to be J.J. Abrams, Ramin Djawadi, Lisa Joy and Jonathan Nolan. However, at the end they encountered with lots of entries (more than 10,000) and the judging process became unclear, and with the first-place winner’s announcement, more controversial.

After a while they even removed the pages related to the competition from their website. However, the whole web were blasted by the critics discussing the results.

Waves of Silence

A short experimental film

Music by Ramtin Didab

Directed by Anita Honart for her MA graduate project at Konstfack University, under supervision of Johanna Billing. The 3-part short experimental musical film pictures the transformation of the creator from being imprisoned under the vein of words, labels, judgements and thoughts to her real “freed, in the present moment” self.

Part I: Iran

This part is picturing a long trip to Iran, the creator’s homeland, or a dive of the current self into “the past”.

It mostly concentrates on the cultural roots which created those judgments and generally “the veins”.

Besides displaying some social problems, the whole part is somehow filled with lives full of color.

One of my original musics titled “In the Paths of Hormuz” is used as the soundtrack for this part. According to the director:

“ There was something special about this piece; its form both created a spiritual and cosmic atmosphere as well as somehow resembling Iran’s cultural spirit because of its semi-folk elements. ”

Part II: Stockholm

In this part we see Stockholm, where the creator currently lives, from her point of view.

A strong contrast is shown in this part, between what we saw in the previous part and the free but cold and full of solitude life in Stockholm.

I scored this part with the music I wrote for it exclusively.

At its ending, the music is faded into another track I had previously made, titled “Leaving the Island”. I saw it a perfect match for the shift which happens in the ending.

Part III: Utopia

The final part displays the liberation stage of the creator’s life, where she goes beyond the judgments of the past, and the current darkness of her life, by coming out of the vein of words and thoughts. The dancing shots are recorded in the Hormuz island.

A music of mine titled “Dusk of Theia” is used for the soundtrack of this part, with some variations to make it more fit to the shots.

The film was played in the konstfack2021 event, and it was selected as one of the top 3 works, among all 15 submissions. The work was also reflected in Sveriges Radio (Sweden’s national publicly funded radio) with lots of complements, with my “In the Paths of Hormuz” track being played in the background, described as “indescribable by any words”!
